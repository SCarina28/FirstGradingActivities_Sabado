Sinigang na Bangus Recipe

Sinigang na Bangus is a type of sour clear broth fish soup. Tamarind is commonly used to flavor the soup, but sometimes other sour fruits and vegetable can be added as well such as mango, miso, guava etc. Bangus is a Filipino word for milkfish that typically lives in tropical waters. It is nice to enjoy sinigang na bangus with warm rice during the rainy seasons. Sinigang na bangus is easy to make and appetizing.

Prep time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 30 minutes
Servings: 3
Author: Vanjo Merano

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs. bangus (milkfish) cleaned and sliced
  • 1pc. 40g pack Knorr Sinigang Sampaloc Mix(original)
  • 1 bunch fresh kangkong leaves
  • 1 bunch sitaw (snake beans) cut into 2 inch
  • 1 bunch okra
  • Long green peper
  • Medium tomato
  • 1 medium yellow onion
  • 1 medium ginger
  • 2 and half tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 quarts water
  • salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat a cooking pot. Pour water into it.
  2. Add tomato,ginger and onion. Let boil.
  3. Add bangus. Cover and cook in medium heat between 8 to 12 minutes.
  4. Add fish sauce and ground black pepper. Stir.
  5. Put the long green pepper into the pot. Add snake beans and okra. Stir. Cover and cook for 5 to 7 minutes.
  6. Add Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc Mix. Stir. Cook for 2 minutes.
  7. Put the kangkong leaves into the pot. Cover the pot and turn the heat off. Let it stay for 5 minutes.
  8. Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve.
